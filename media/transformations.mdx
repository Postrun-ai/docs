title: "Transformations"
description: "What Postrun adjusts in your file so each platform accepts it — and what it deliberately leaves untouched."
---

## Images

- **Resizes** down to the platform's dimension limit, keeping the aspect ratio.
- **Converts** to the format the platform expects — JPEG for the social platforms, PNG where transparency needs to be preserved.
- **Recompresses** to stay under the platform's file-size cap.
- **Rasterizes** formats the platforms can't accept at all (AVIF, TIFF, SVG) into a standard raster.

## Video

- **Re-encodes the codec only when a target requires it.** X accepts H.264 only, so an HEVC clip (the iOS default) becomes H.264 when X is a target. Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and TikTok all accept HEVC — a file going only to those platforms is re-wrapped without re-encoding: fast, and with no quality loss.
- **Moves the MP4 index to the front** (the moov atom / faststart), so playback starts before the full download completes.
- **Rounds odd pixel dimensions to even** (H.264 requires it) and **clamps frame rate** to the platform's maximum.

GIFs and documents are validated against platform limits but not transformed.

## What Postrun never changes

Postrun adjusts the technical envelope — format, codec, size — **never your content**. It doesn't crop, trim, or restage your media. If a clip is longer than a platform allows, or an image's aspect ratio falls outside a platform's range, Postrun surfaces a typed error rather than silently cutting the content. See [Media Response](/media/media-response).

## Knowing what was adjusted

Every adjustment appears in `per_platform[target].warnings` — for example, that a video was re-encoded or an image recompressed. The rendition is ready to use; the warning records what changed. See [Media Response → Problems surface in three places](/media/media-response#problems-surface-in-three-places).
