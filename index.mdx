Postrun is one API for publishing to social platforms and running ads across
Meta and Google. Namespaced, native payloads — no lowest-common-denominator
normalization — wrapped in a single envelope: one auth model, typed errors,
idempotency, dry-run, audit logs, and webhooks that behave the same on every
platform.

It's built to be called **directly from your frontend**. Your backend mints a
short-lived, scoped token from a secret key; the browser calls Postrun with it.
No proxy, no database of your own for the social and ads domain — Postrun is the
system of record.

## What you can do

- **Publish** to X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook Pages, and TikTok from one
  request, with media handled for you (resumable uploads, platform-ready
  renditions).
- **Run ads** on Meta and Google — read insights and make guarded writes
  (pause, budget, duplicate) behind idempotency, dry-run, and an audit log.
- **Store the domain** — profiles, connections, drafts, schedules, and media
  live on Postrun, queryable by your own `external_id` and `metadata`.

## The envelope

Every endpoint shares the same contract, and that consistency is the product:

- **One auth model** — `Authorization: Bearer <credential>`, either a secret
  `pr_` key or a scoped frontend token.
- **Typed errors** — a closed [code registry](/errors/overview) over an
  RFC 9457 problem body, plus a render-ready `display_error` on every failure.
- **Idempotency** — send an `Idempotency-Key` on any write; retries are safe.
- **Dry-run** — preview a write's outcome without touching the platform.
- **Per-account rate limits** — shared across every key and token, split by
  read and write.
