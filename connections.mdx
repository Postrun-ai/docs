A **connection** links one of your [profiles](/storage) to a single account on a
platform — an X handle, a Facebook Page, an Instagram account, a Google Ads
account. Once connected, that profile can post or run ads through it. A profile can
hold many connections, one per account, across many platforms.

You never handle a platform password or access token. The account's owner authorizes
access through the platform's own OAuth, and Postrun holds and refreshes that grant.

## How a connection comes to life

Connecting takes two steps, because **one login can expose more than one account**:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Grant access">
    The user signs in to the platform and authorizes access. This produces a grant —
    but not yet a specific account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an account">
    One grant can reach several accounts — a Facebook login manages multiple Pages, a
    Google login has multiple ad accounts. You pick the one this connection is for. If
    the grant reaches exactly one account, this step happens automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The result is an **active** connection bound to one specific account. Until an account
is bound, the connection sits in `pending`.

## What a connection carries

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `profile_id` | The profile this connection belongs to. |
| `platform` | `x`, `linkedin`, `instagram`, `facebook_page`, `tiktok` (social) or `google_ads`, `meta_ads` (ads). |
| `kind` | `posting` or `ads` — derived from the platform. |
| `status` | `pending` (granted, no account bound yet) · `active` · `needs_reauth` (the grant expired). |
| `external_account_id` / `external_account_name` | The platform account it's bound to. |
| `username` / `avatar_url` / `profile_url` | Cached display for social accounts, so you can render the connection without another call. |

## Connect from your frontend

The entire flow runs **in your app**: the user clicks "Connect", an OAuth popup opens
(no redirect away from your page), they pick an account if there's more than one, and
the connection lands `active`. No backend of your own, and no platform tokens to store
— Postrun mints a short-lived connect session and the SDK drives the rest.

If you're not using React, every connect session also returns a `hosted_connect_url` —
a Postrun-hosted page that runs the same flow.
